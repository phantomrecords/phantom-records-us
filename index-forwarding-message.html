Thank you for all the years of visiting Phantom Records GP at this web address: https://phantomrecords.com.

I first built that site in 2004, before I graduated with my degree in Multimedia Design Science—a field that combines video editing, animation, web design, and graphic design. Back then, I didn’t yet know how to fully leverage the world’s history of visual design and merge it with modern computer science for the web.

Earlier, I had already launched a very successful startup overseas—https://gamescape.co.nz—but that market was small, smaller than the city of San Francisco, and it wasn’t difficult to succeed there. Since 1997, it has taken me until now, in 2025, to learn how to become the best at what I love and to be recognized for it while making a living.

Doing business in the United States is tough. The U.S. attracts the smartest competitors, but it also attracts the smartest bad actors—people who, when they fail, resort to harming businesses through crime, often without consequences. That means every business has to fight not just competitors, but also those working against it unfairly. In many ways, business in America is inherently “cutthroat.” And when emergencies strike and local law enforcement falters, real life can be just as cutthroat. I learned that lesson during the U.S. pandemic of 2020, and I’m stronger for it.
