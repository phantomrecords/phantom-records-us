The Origin of Phantom Records


Phantom Records was founded in 1989 as a Doing Business As (DBA) entity in Berkeley, California. At the time, Michael Widener had just graduated from high school with honors and a 4.5 GPA. His mother provided him with the ten dollars needed to register the business, along with additional funds for a certified birth certificate, setting the foundation for what would become Phantom Records.

Michael had been motivated throughout high school by his father’s promise: if he graduated with top grades, he would be rewarded with a house. However, upon achieving this goal, he discovered that his father had only been joking. The disappointment was immense.

To help ease his frustration and sadness, his mother assured him that she would find a way to support his future, though she made it clear that sending him to university was financially impossible—even though his grades surpassed those of his siblings.

A Difficult Transition

Michael struggled with the reality of his situation. Scholarships were not on his radar, as he had never been informed about them or applied for any. He also had not taken Advanced Placement (AP) exams, as his family could not afford the testing fees.

On top of that, his parents needed someone to stay home and look after the house while they were working or traveling. With no immediate path to university, Michael agreed to take on this responsibility.

But this decision came at a cost.

While his high school friends left for university, including his two closest best friends, Michael found himself completely alone for the first time in his life—no friends, no brothers, no daily social interactions.

The Birth of Phantom Records

Recognizing the toll this isolation could take on Michael’s mental and emotional well-being, his mother stepped in with a solution—she encouraged him to start a business. She hoped that Phantom Records would provide him with a sense of purpose, direction, and structure, filling the void left by his absent friends and siblings.

Rather than distracting them from their challenging first year at university, Michael poured his energy into building Phantom Records, laying the foundation for what would eventually grow into a recognized music business.
